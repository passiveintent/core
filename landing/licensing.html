Open-source core vs enterprise

Choose based on how complex the buyer journey really is.

The AGPL core is deliberately narrow: one live session, one site, one tab. Enterprise widens that model for repeat visits, connected properties, comparison-heavy research, and campaign volatility, while keeping the same privacy-first posture.

Visit scope
One live visit → Repeat visits

Stay with the decision when a customer comes back later instead of starting from zero each time.

Property scope
One site → Connected properties

Carry context across your brand site, store, and checkout flows without creating a surveillance stack.

Research behavior
One tab → Full comparison journey

Understand buyers who open multiple tabs, compare options, disappear, and return with a different level of intent.

Campaign resilience
Stable traffic → Launch and promo spikes

Keep the signal useful through launches, flash sales, and other abrupt shifts in buyer behavior.

AGPL open-source core

Best for proving the loop inside one active decision window.

Use the core when you want a clean, transparent, privacy-first engine for in-session intervention without commercial rollout complexity.

  • Great for open-source adoption, evaluation, pilots, and internal exploration
  • Designed for one site, one tab, and one live visit at a time
  • Deterministic intent math your product and engineering teams can inspect
  • Zero-egress by default for teams starting with the cleanest privacy posture
Enterprise commercial pack Scaled journey coverage

Built for revenue journeys that stretch across time, sites, and tabs.

Choose enterprise when a single isolated session is no longer enough to explain the customer’s real buying journey.

  • Carry intent across repeat visits, connected properties, and multi-tab research
  • Add premium signals for high-value hesitation, comparison shopping, churn risk, and coercion patterns
  • Stay resilient during launches, promotions, and flash-sale volatility
  • Validate access offline and fall back cleanly if enterprise features are unavailable
Zero-egress preserved No phone-home Graceful fallback

Operationally: Enterprise extends the core rather than replacing it. It keeps the privacy boundary intact, validates offline, and drops back to the open-source experience if enterprise access is not active.

Commercial use

You need a commercial license if PassiveIntent ships inside a proprietary product, runs in a network service without releasing the full app under AGPLv3, or is resold or white-labeled inside an analytics or AdTech product.
